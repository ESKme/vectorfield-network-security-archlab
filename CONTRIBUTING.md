# Contributing to VECTORFIELD

Thank you for your interest in the VECTORFIELD enterprise network security architecture study.

This repository represents a **methodological enterprise cybersecurity architecture study**.
The goal is to make the design logic transparent and to support learning, discussion,
and further exploration of enterprise network security architecture.

Contributions are welcome when they improve clarity, technical accuracy,
or the educational value of the project.

---

## Types of Contributions

Contributions may include:

- corrections of technical inaccuracies
- improvements in documentation clarity
- additional architectural explanations
- references to relevant standards or research
- discussion of alternative architecture approaches
- suggestions for improving traceability or validation logic

The focus of this repository is **methodology and architectural reasoning**, not
product comparison or tool promotion.

---

## Contribution Guidelines

When proposing changes, please ensure that contributions:

- maintain the **traceability-driven architecture logic**
- respect the **Single Source of Truth (SSoT) concept**
- preserve the **default-deny security philosophy**
- avoid introducing unnecessary architectural complexity
- remain consistent with the study's methodological approach

Substantial architectural changes should be accompanied by a clear explanation
of the reasoning and trade-offs involved.

---

## Discussion and Suggestions

If you have ideas or questions, you can:

- open a **GitHub Issue** to start a discussion
- propose improvements through a **Pull Request**
- suggest additional documentation or explanatory material

Constructive discussion and professional exchange are encouraged.
